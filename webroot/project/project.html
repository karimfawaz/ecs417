My Project: Brain 2.0

Deciding that my future lied in Technology was never a discovery. From an early age, I enjoyed finding efficient solutions to everyday problems. On a personal scale, I tried to optimise the way I executed my daily tasks from a time and energy consumption standpoint. It was through trial and error that I had discovered the dopamine rush that came from failing numerous times before finally finding the best possible solution. Video games on mobile and console platforms were a prime source of entertainment during my childhood and teenage years. I enjoyed playing the games but did not understand how they worked. When I turned 15, I decided to build my own endless runner 2D game. It was through this project that I had discovered the difference between consuming the internet and actually using it to my advantage. I had used the Unity2D documentation pages and tutorials to start building 2D Arcade Games, using C Sharp.
